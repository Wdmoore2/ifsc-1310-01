    Thoughts on Web Accessiblity

    September 25th, 2024

    Web accessibility is what allows our website to be accessible to everyone. It's important because first of all it is legal requirement. But beyond that it is the right thing to do. I have appreciated how much accessibility has come up in my college experience right now. One lecture in my first year experience class the other day visiting professor William Cox highlighted that one of the issues with solving problems is the laws that are in place, it is not really enough. He stated as an example that to comply with ADA requirements to a building you have to have a ramp to make it wheelchair accessible. But it does not indicate where this ramp has to be placed. It could be placed where the user has to go all the way around to the back of the building. True accessibility gives everyone the same access and opportunity.

    From the video: "Accessibility in the modern web" it took my understanding of web accessibility even further. My limited understanding thought our website would just make navigation and accessibility harder for some people. But I came to realize It has the potential to make it impossible to accomplish the same task "click the button" with no option allowing you to press the spacebar on a keyboard as an example. I find this an incredibly irritating thing. I think back to times on the web I thought something was of poor design. Example: date of birth calendar that makes you click though every single month you have been alive to get to your date of birth. That is frustrating but if I was not able to fill that in at all or click on it I would be really upset. That is taking a task I am perfectly capable of doing on my own but due to poor design I now have to take extra steps of reaching out to someone. I have to consider whether the product or service is even worth it at that point.

    The video series from w3c on "website accessible prospectives" videos were super helpful in easily highlighting what the issue or limitation might be and why it is thus important to make sure that the accessibility is in place.

    It had videos on the following topics:
    • Keyboard Compatibility
    • Video Captions
    • Colors with good contrast.
    • Customizable text
    • Clear layout and design
    • Speech recognition
    • Understandable content
    • Large links, buttons and controls.
    • Notifications and feedback.

    From that list I really could relate to the clear layout and design. It helps me understand that we all have a sort of expectation on how things are layed out. It reminds me when I worked as a server and was told that the salt is always on the right and pepper on the left for those that are visually impaired. They have an expectation of where the salt should be. I can appreciate that while I have not always had the understanding of how things should be it really does make sense that things have more thought and meaning put into it. So from this assignment I have found the ideas we have been talking about in class makes sense: It has legal implications, it's the right thing to do and makes financial sense for a company regardless if someone needs a specific accessibility feature or not.

    Will Moore | Web Technologies 1310 Fall 2024
